Papaitan

Description

Adobo is a quintessential Filipino dish featuring meat (commonly chicken or pork) braised in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. The cooking process involves simmering the ingredients until the meat is tender and the flavors meld together, resulting in a savory and tangy stew often served with steamed rice. Due to its vinegar content, adobo has a long shelf life and was traditionally packed for travelers.

Ingredients

  • chicken
  • soy sauce
  • vinegar
  • garlic
  • bay leaves
  • pepper

Steps

  1. Heat atsuete oil in a pot. Sear your meat until brown.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, and onion. Cook until fragrant.
  3. Add soy sauce, vinegar, and water. Simmer until the sauce reduces.
  4. Season with cracked pepper, bay leaves, patis and brown sugar. Serve immediately.
